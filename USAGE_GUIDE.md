# NRR Predictor - Usage Guide (Updated)

## What Changed? 🔧

**Problem Fixed:** The bowling predictor was estimating your team's previous match statistics using a generic 8 runs/over baseline, which led to inaccurate predictions.

**Solution:** The predictor now asks for your team's **actual cumulative statistics** from all previous matches for accurate calculations.

## How to Use the Bowling Predictor (Defend Mode) 📊

### Step 1: Gather Your Team's Cumulative Stats from Previous Matches

You need your team's **total runs and overs across ALL completed matches**, not just the current NRR.

**Where to find this data:**
- Official tournament websites (ICC, IPL, etc.)
- Cricket stats websites (ESPNcricinfo, Cricbuzz)
- Tournament scorecards

**What you need:**
1. **Total Runs Scored** - Sum of all runs your team scored in all completed matches
2. **Total Overs Faced** - Sum of all overs your team faced in all completed matches
3. **Total Runs Conceded** - Sum of all runs your team conceded in all completed matches
4. **Total Overs Bowled** - Sum of all overs your team bowled in all completed matches

### Step 2: Enter Your Current Match Data

After batting first, enter:
- **Your Runs Scored** - Runs you scored in the current match
- **Your Overs Faced** - Overs you faced in the current match

### Step 3: Enter Target Information

- **Target NRR to Beat** - The NRR of the team you need to surpass (e.g., New Zealand's +1.390)
- **Target Team Name** - Name of that team (optional, for display)
- **Opponent Team Name** - The team you're currently playing against (e.g., Sri Lanka)

### Step 4: Get Your Defense Strategy! 🎯

The calculator will show you:
- Maximum runs you can allow at different overs
- Your projected NRR for each scenario
- Best and worst-case defense strategies

## Why This Matters 🎯

**Before:** Estimated stats led to inaccurate predictions (could be off by 30-40 runs)

**After:** Using actual cumulative stats gives precise, reliable predictions that match real-world scenarios

## Tips for Accuracy 💡

1. **Use Official Stats:** Get data from official tournament sources
2. **Include All Matches:** Make sure your cumulative totals include ALL completed matches
3. **Check Overs Format:** Enter overs as decimal (e.g., 19.4 overs = 19 overs + 4 balls)
4. **Verify Your NRR:** Calculate (Total Runs Scored / Total Overs Faced) - (Total Runs Conceded / Total Overs Bowled) to verify your cumulative stats

---

## Finding Cumulative Stats Online 🌐

### ESPNcricinfo
1. Go to the tournament page
2. Click on "Points Table" or "Standings"
3. Click on your team name
4. View "Match Results" with detailed scorecards
5. Add up runs and overs from each match

### Cricbuzz
1. Go to the tournament section
2. Select "Points Table"
3. Click on your team
4. View individual match scorecards
5. Calculate cumulative totals

3. Add up each match's runs and overs manually
